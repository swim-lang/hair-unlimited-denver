# Hair Unlimited of Denver

## Website research and content strategy brief

Prepared July 20, 2026 for the redesign and Paper design phase.

## Executive direction

Hair Unlimited does not need a large website. It needs a small, exceptionally clear one that makes a private consultation easy to understand and easy to book.

The strongest position is not "another hair-loss clinic." It is:

> Denver's experienced, independent studio for private, custom non-surgical hair replacement.

The business already has the proof for that position:

- Serving Denver since 1989.
- Custom hair systems for men and women.
- Private, no-pressure consultations.
- A dedicated stylist and long-term client relationships.
- Real before-and-after photography.
- Reviews that repeatedly mention natural color matching, undetectable results, empathy, flexibility, and care over decades.

The redesign should make Hair Unlimited feel contemporary without making Ralph feel like a corporate clinic or a trend-driven barber. The right tone is confident, discreet, direct, human, and experienced.

The proposed site is four editorial pages plus a lightweight booking utility:

1. Home
2. Hair Systems
3. Results
4. The Studio
5. Book / Manage Appointment as an embedded or linked utility, not a fifth content-heavy page

## 1. Current-state audit

### What the current site communicates

The live one-page site describes Hair Unlimited as a full-service salon and non-surgical hair replacement center for men and women. It says the company has operated since 1989 and emphasizes custom systems, custom molds, dedicated styling, private consultations, active lifestyles, and thousands of clients. It lists 7535 E. Hampden Ave., Building 2, Suite 502, Denver, CO 80231 and uses `(303) 868-1977` as the main number.

Source: [Hair Unlimited of Denver](https://www.hairunlimitedco.com/)

### What is worth keeping

- "Since 1989" is the clearest trust marker and should appear near the top of every major journey.
- "Private consultation" is both a practical benefit and an emotional reassurance.
- Custom fit, color, texture, density, and styling are more persuasive than generic "confidence" language.
- Dedicated care is a credible boutique advantage over larger corporate providers.
- The eight existing before-and-after sets are technically usable at 1200 x 900 pixels and are much stronger than the current stock-style hero photography.
- The existing Tony video is still available on YouTube. The issue is its presentation: the current page places it behind a modal/autoplay treatment rather than introducing it as an intentional client story.

### What is failing

#### Conversion and usability

- There is no meaningful navigation.
- There is no online booking or appointment request flow.
- Phone number and address are plain text rather than tap-to-call and map links.
- The email address is split across a dead `#` link and underlined text.
- Every before-and-after card links to `#`, which jumps the user rather than opening a larger image or client story.
- The mobile hero headline overflows and is visibly clipped.
- The video depends on an old modal/autoplay implementation. Even though the YouTube asset exists, the experience is fragile and gives the impression that the video is broken.
- There is no clear path for current clients who simply need to rebook maintenance.

#### Content and trust

- The opening message is category language rather than a compelling promise.
- The page repeats broad confidence claims but does not explain the actual process, timing, maintenance, cost variables, or what happens at a first appointment.
- It does not introduce Ralph, the team, or the studio despite the business being relationship-driven.
- It gives equal visual weight to generic photography and real transformations; the real work should be the hero evidence.
- The copyright remains 2017, which reinforces the feeling that the site is unattended.
- The site says "thousands of happy clients" and makes other absolute statements without visible evidence. These should be verified or rewritten more precisely.

#### Search and technical quality

- The page contains an extremely long, typo-heavy meta-keyword list, including outdated SEO tactics and irrelevant repetitions.
- The main H1 appears near the end of the page rather than anchoring the opening content.
- There is no visible canonical URL, Open Graph metadata, local-business structured data, or useful page hierarchy.
- Multiple images use the alt text `image`, wasting an accessibility and search opportunity.
- One page cannot target the distinct questions behind "hair systems Denver," "non-surgical hair replacement Denver," "hair system maintenance," "men's hair replacement," and "women's hair replacement."

### The brochure

The downloadable brochure is a scanned two-page laser-hair-therapy piece created in 2009. It uses the older `(303) 755-4448` number and contains claims such as an 85–93% outcome range, "cessation of hair loss," and broad statements about clinical proof and safety.

It should not be republished as-is. Any health, regrowth, laser, or safety claim should be freshly verified against the exact product or service currently offered. FTC guidance says objective health and safety claims must be truthful, non-misleading, and supported by competent and reliable scientific evidence.

Sources: [current brochure](https://www.hairunlimitedco.com/brochure.pdf), [FTC Health Products Compliance Guidance](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/health-products-compliance-guidance)

### Business-information conflicts to resolve before launch

- Current website phone: `(303) 868-1977`
- Older brochure and directory phone: `(303) 755-4448`
- Birdeye hours: Monday–Friday, 9:00 a.m.–6:00 p.m.; closed weekends
- Yellow Pages hours: Monday–Friday, 9:00 a.m.–5:00 p.m.; Saturday, 8:00 a.m.–2:00 p.m.
- Address is consistently listed as 7535 E. Hampden Ave., Building 2, Suite 502.

The live site, Google Business Profile, directories, social accounts, and new booking tool should all use one verified phone number, one set of hours, and one naming convention.

Sources: [Birdeye listing](https://reviews.birdeye.com/hair-unlimited-of-denver-955011136), [Yellow Pages listing](https://www.yellowpages.com/denver-co/mip/hair-unlimited-of-denver-inc-13756875)

## 3. Market and competitor research

### HairClub Denver: the corporate benchmark

HairClub presents a wide menu—regrowth, non-surgical systems, surgery, scalp micropigmentation, and laser products—along with a free consultation and substantial national proof. Its advantage is breadth and operational polish. Its weakness for this opportunity is the feeling of a large system with proprietary programs.

Hair Unlimited should not imitate that breadth. It should contrast it with continuity, independence, privacy, and care from people who know the client personally.

Source: [HairClub Denver](https://www.hairclub.com/locations/united-states/colorado/greenwood-village/denver/)

### Studio 20, Fort Collins: the strongest nearby digital competitor

Studio 20 has the clearest modern local presentation. It is male-forward, visually bold, private, specific about services, and direct about booking. Its site separates a free consultation from haircut/system-maintenance booking and explains a four-step process.

Useful lessons:

- Lead with a memorable, plain-English promise.
- Make consultation and returning-client actions distinct.
- Explain the process before asking for commitment.
- Make privacy tangible: one room, one client, no awkward waiting.
- Treat maintenance and styling education as part of the service, not an afterthought.

Do not copy its black-and-gold luxury/barber aesthetic or its aggressive tone. Hair Unlimited has a different strength: almost four decades of calm, personal care for a broader client base.

Source: [Studio 20 by John Scott](https://www.studio20byjohnscott.com/)

### Denver Toupee Club: proof of a younger local audience

Denver Toupee Club uses casual, accessible language and explicitly tries to make hair replacement feel normal and enjoyable. It has a consultation CTA, but its information architecture and writing are still limited.

This shows an opening for Hair Unlimited to feel youthful without using slang or abandoning its experience.

Source: [Denver Chop Shop / Denver Toupee Club](https://www.denverchopshop.com/)

### Sly Hair, New York: editorial privacy and founder-led care

Sly Hair frames the experience as a private studio, emphasizes one specialist and one client at a time, gives prospects a realistic maintenance conversation, and uses editorial hierarchy instead of clinic graphics. It also separates questions about cost, maintenance, and alternatives.

Useful lessons:

- Privacy is not a footnote; it can be the organizing idea.
- Founder-led continuity is valuable.
- A consultation should promise a straight answer about fit, lifestyle, maintenance, and next steps—not a sales trap.
- Real studio and transformation photography can carry the brand.

Source: [Sly Hair](https://www.slyhair.com/category/all-products)

### HairPlaceNYC and Hair Systems Waterloo: clarity about ownership and upkeep

These sites answer the practical questions prospects actually have: what a system is, whether it looks natural, whether it stays secure during exercise and showering, how often maintenance is needed, how long a system may last, what a fitting includes, and whether pricing is transparent.

Hair Unlimited should answer the same questions, but only with Ralph's verified service details. Competitor numbers should not be copied as Hair Unlimited promises.

Sources: [HairPlaceNYC](https://www.hairplacenyc.com/mens-hair-systems/), [Hair Systems Waterloo](https://hairsystemswaterloo.co.uk/)

### The market opening

Hair Unlimited can credibly occupy the space between:

- Large, multi-solution corporate clinics.
- Young barber-led hair-system studios.
- DIY/e-commerce hair systems with no local continuity.

The opportunity is an experienced Denver specialist with modern access:

> Old-school care, without the old-school friction.

That thought is strategically right even if it never appears verbatim on the site.

## 4. Audience and decision journey

### Primary audience

Men roughly 25–60 experiencing a receding hairline, thinning crown, advanced loss, or a disappointing previous solution. They want to look like themselves, not like they are wearing "a piece." Many are comparing a system with surgery, medication, shaving, or continuing to hide the problem.

### Important secondary audiences

- Current hair-system wearers who have moved to Denver or need a better provider.
- Existing Hair Unlimited clients booking maintenance or salon service.
- Women experiencing thinning, alopecia, medical hair loss, or poor results from an earlier piece.
- People with limited donor hair or those who do not want surgery.

### Questions the site must answer

1. Will it look natural up close?
2. Will people know?
3. Is the appointment private?
4. What exactly is a hair system?
5. What happens at the first consultation?
6. How is color, texture, density, hairline, and style matched?
7. Do I need to shave my head?
8. Can I shower, sleep, exercise, swim, and travel normally?
9. How often will I need maintenance?
10. How long does a system last?
11. What does it cost initially and over time?
12. How soon can I have a finished result?
13. Can Hair Unlimited service a system purchased elsewhere?
14. Are there options for partial loss, a crown, or more extensive coverage?
15. What happens if my hair changes over time?

The new site does not need to answer every edge case in the hero. It needs to show that Hair Unlimited understands these anxieties and will discuss them honestly in a private consultation.

## 5. Positioning and voice

### Positioning statement

For Denver clients who want a natural solution to hair loss without surgery or a corporate-clinic experience, Hair Unlimited provides custom hair systems and ongoing one-to-one care in a private studio, backed by experience since 1989.

### Brand pillars

1. **Natural by design** — fit, color, texture, density, hairline, cut, and lifestyle considered together.
2. **Private by default** — discreet consultation and service, presented as a real operational promise.
3. **Personal over corporate** — continuity with a stylist who knows the client and their history.
4. **Experience without pressure** — decades of pattern recognition, explained plainly.
5. **Built for real life** — care and maintenance framed honestly, with no fantasy claims.

### Voice

- Direct, warm, concise, and specific.
- Masculine-leaning but not macho; inclusive of women without turning the brand generic.
- Calm enough for a sensitive decision.
- Never clinical unless discussing a verified clinical service.
- Never euphemistic or ashamed of the words "hair system" and "hair replacement."
- Avoid tired lines such as "be the new you," "don't settle," "life-changing," and repeated generic confidence promises.
- Prefer observable benefits: private room, precise match, one dedicated stylist, a clear maintenance plan, immediate visual coverage, easy rebooking.

### Headline territories for design exploration

These are directions, not approved final copy:

- Hair that looks like it was always yours.
- Look like yourself again.
- Custom hair. Private care. Denver since 1989.
- A natural result starts with a precise fit.
- Hair loss is personal. Your solution should be too.
- Your hair should not be the first thing people notice.
- Decades of experience. One result that feels like you.

## 6. Four-page information architecture

### Global header

**Wordmark** | Hair Systems | Results | The Studio | **Book a Private Consultation**

Utility options:

- `Call / Text` on desktop.
- A sticky `Book` action on mobile.
- `Questions` can deep-link to the FAQ section on Hair Systems rather than becoming a fifth page.

### Page 1: Home

**Job:** Establish relevance, prove the difference, and move a new prospect toward a private consultation.

Recommended flow:

1. Editorial hero with a specific promise, Denver/since-1989 context, and primary booking CTA.
2. Short recognition block: thinning, receding, a crown that no longer feels like you, an old system that is not working.
3. Plain-language solution introduction.
4. Proof strip: `Since 1989`, `Custom fitted`, `Private consultations`—only verified facts.
5. Featured real transformation with a client story or testimonial.
6. "Boutique by choice" section explaining dedicated care and continuity.
7. Simple four-step process.
8. Men and women / range-of-needs section.
9. Selected FAQ preview.
10. Strong final consultation CTA with call/text alternative.

Suggested hero structure:

- Eyebrow: `PRIVATE HAIR REPLACEMENT / DENVER SINCE 1989`
- H1 territory: `Hair that looks like it was always yours.`
- Support: `Custom non-surgical hair systems, precisely matched and fitted in a private Denver studio.`
- Primary CTA: `Book a private consultation`
- Secondary CTA: `See real results`

### Page 2: Hair Systems

**Job:** Replace mystery with practical clarity.

Recommended flow:

1. Large opening statement: `A natural result starts with a precise fit.`
2. What a modern hair system is—and what it is not.
3. Who it may suit: receding, thinning, crown loss, alopecia, extensive loss, current system wearers.
4. What gets customized: base/coverage, hairline, color, texture, density, length, cut, attachment, maintenance plan.
5. Process: consultation → measure/match → system preparation/order → private fitting and cut → ongoing care.
6. Daily life: styling, sleeping, exercise, showering, travel—using only Ralph-approved specifics.
7. Service menu: new system, fitting/cut-in, maintenance/rebond, salon service, and any women's/medical/laser offerings that are confirmed current.
8. Transparent FAQ covering timing, maintenance, longevity, cost variables, and what to bring.
9. Consultation CTA.

### Page 3: Results

**Job:** Make the quality visible and credible.

Recommended flow:

1. Large title and a short statement about real clients and real work.
2. Gallery of the eight current before-and-after sets, upgraded to open properly.
3. Two or three deeper client stories with need, approach, finished style, and lived result.
4. Short review excerpts attached to relevant transformations when permission allows.
5. Optional Tony video, presented with a poster image, title, duration, captions/transcript, and manual play.
6. Results-vary / consent language as appropriate.
7. CTA to discuss the viewer's own goals privately.

Gallery guidance:

- Keep result imagery in accurate color because color and blend are evidence.
- Do not over-retouch skin or hairlines.
- Avoid using first name, age, and occupation unless there is clear permission and a narrative reason.
- Write useful alt text and allow keyboard-accessible enlargement.
- Add newer work over time with consistent camera angle, lighting, crop, and background.

### Page 4: The Studio

**Job:** Turn an unknown business into a trusted person and place.

Recommended flow:

1. `One Denver studio. Decades of care.`
2. Ralph's founder story and why he has stayed in the work since 1989.
3. His personal understanding of hair systems, if confirmed and approved.
4. The boutique promise: privacy, continuity, flexibility, honest recommendations.
5. Team portraits and short bios if other stylists are active.
6. Studio photography: entrance, private room, chair, tools/materials, consultation moment.
7. Verified address, hours, parking/access instructions, map, accessibility details.
8. Call, text, email, and booking CTA.

### Booking utility

The booking experience should split immediately:

#### New clients

`Book a private consultation`

- Suggested duration: 30 minutes, subject to Ralph's confirmation.
- State whether it is free or paid.
- Collect name, phone, email, new/current system wearer, general goal, preferred contact method, and optional private notes.
- Explain what will happen and what the client should bring.
- Send confirmation, reminder, directions/parking, and a simple reschedule link.

#### Current clients

`Book your next visit`

- Show only verified returning-client services and realistic durations.
- Examples to confirm: maintenance/rebond, haircut and blend, new-system fitting, consultation/check-in.
- Allow rescheduling within the business's policy.
- Keep `Call or text Ralph` visible for anything unusual.

This is the initial "client space." A custom account portal would add cost without solving the immediate problem. A good scheduling platform already gives clients confirmation, reminders, and self-service changes.

## 7. Booking-platform recommendation

### Recommended: Square Appointments

Square is a better fit than Calendly if the goal is to reduce manual scheduling for both consultations and ongoing salon/system services. It supports different services and durations, online booking, an embedded widget or button, confirmations and reminders, calendar controls, deposits/no-show policies, client profiles, waitlists, staff calendars, and future multi-location management.

It also lets the business require acceptance for appointment requests if Ralph wants to retain control during the transition.

Sources: [Square Appointments features and pricing](https://squareup.com/us/en/appointments/pricing), [Square online booking setup](https://squareup.com/help/us/en/article/5355-set-up-online-booking-with-square-appointments), [accept or decline appointments](https://squareup.com/help/us/en/article/8444-accept-or-decline-appointments)

### Calendly: acceptable for consultation-only scheduling

Calendly can embed on the site, sync calendars, collect form answers, take payments through integrations, and automate reminders. It is strong for one appointment type, such as a 30-minute consultation. It is less naturally suited to a growing menu of salon services, recurring maintenance, client records, deposits, chairs/staff, or multi-location service operations.

Source: [Calendly features](https://calendly.com/features/)

### Low-risk rollout

1. Put only new-client consultations online for two weeks.
2. Keep all bookings as request/approval if needed.
3. Add automated confirmation and a 24-hour reminder.
4. Add the two or three most common returning-client services after durations and buffers are proven.
5. Keep call/text available for exceptions.
6. Review no-shows, reschedules, and phone volume after 30 days.

## 9. Visual and editorial direction for Paper

### Core look

- Black, white, and warm off-white rather than sterile bright white.
- Helvetica/Neue Haas/Helvetica Now-style sans serif as the primary system.
- Large, tightly composed headlines; quieter body copy; small uppercase utility labels.
- Strong grid, asymmetry, generous whitespace, and visible rules/dividers.
- Minimal animation. The work and people should move the page, not decorative effects.
- A typographic wordmark is enough: `HAIR UNLIMITED` with `DENVER` or `SINCE 1989` as a secondary line. Avoid hair-follicle icons, swooshes, gradients, and faux-medical symbols.

### Photography

Photography should be the visual identity.

Priority subjects:

1. Real finished clients—portraits, profiles, hairlines, crowns, and natural movement.
2. Standardized before-and-after pairs.
3. Ralph in the studio, not posed like a doctor.
4. Quiet, close details: matching color, cutting, hands, texture, tools, mirror, private room.
5. Studio arrival and entrance so a nervous first-time client knows what to expect.

Use black-and-white editorial imagery for brand atmosphere, but keep transformation images in honest color. The current mountain hero is attractive but generic; a real client or Ralph portrait would build more trust.

### Writing rhythm

The design should alternate:

- One large human statement.
- One short explanatory paragraph.
- A compact list or numbered process.
- A full-bleed or paired photograph.
- A precise proof point or quote.
- A clear next action.

This creates the varied web-writing rhythm requested without producing a long wall of marketing copy.

## 11. Ralph interview / discovery questions

These questions must be answered before final copy is locked:

### History and positioning

1. Did Hair Unlimited open in 1989, and has Ralph led it continuously?
2. Why did Ralph enter hair replacement?
3. Has he personally worn a hair system, and is he comfortable sharing that story?
4. Approximately how many active clients does the studio serve? Is there documentation for any lifetime-client figure?
5. What does Ralph believe he does better than HairClub, online systems, transplant clinics, and younger barber-led studios?

### Services

6. Which services are active today: custom bonded systems, clip-ins, toppers, full wigs, partial/crown systems, cut-ins, maintenance, salon cuts, color, laser therapy, regrowth products, women's solutions?
7. Which services are no longer offered?
8. Does Hair Unlimited work on systems purchased elsewhere?
9. Are systems custom-made, modified stock, or both?
10. What materials and hair types are used?
11. What gets measured or molded during a consultation?

### Process and expectations

12. What happens in a first consultation and how long does it take?
13. Is it free? Is there any deposit?
14. What is the typical time from consultation to finished fitting?
15. How long does the fitting/cut appointment take?
16. What maintenance cadence does Ralph actually recommend?
17. What changes based on lifestyle, scalp, attachment method, or system type?
18. What can clients safely do at home, and what should be handled in the studio?
19. What are realistic cost ranges or starting prices Ralph is willing to publish?

### Operations

20. Which phone number, hours, and contact method are current?
21. Does Ralph prefer calls, texts, or booking requests?
22. Which appointment types should be self-bookable?
23. How much buffer does each appointment require?
24. Should new appointments auto-confirm or require approval?
25. What is the cancellation/no-show policy?
26. Who besides Ralph needs calendar access now or in a future second location?

### Proof and photography

27. Which current clients could be invited into a new photo shoot?
28. Which three client stories best show the range of problems solved?
29. Are the current eight before-and-after clients still reachable for updated consent?
30. Which reviews can be attributed by first name, initials, or anonymously?

## 12. Recommended next move

Before designing full pages in Paper:

1. Confirm the facts and service model with Ralph using the interview above.
2. Choose the booking platform and define the initial appointment types.
3. Select the strongest four to six current transformations.
4. Approve one positioning statement and one hero-copy direction.
5. Design the Home page and the shared header/footer first.
6. Use the approved system to build Hair Systems, Results, and The Studio.

The central idea should remain consistent through every phase:

> Hair Unlimited is not trying to become a bigger clinic online. It is making its private, personal expertise easier to see—and much easier to access.
